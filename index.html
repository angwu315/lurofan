Taiwanese Braised Pork Rice

Braised pork over rice or Lu you fan (滷肉飯) is one of the most beloved Taiwanese comfort food. Similar dish can be dated back to Zhou Dynasty (1100 - 221 BC) and has since developed into countless regional variations. It’s one of my favourite dish from my country and this is the recipe I used to re-created them in Vancouver!

You will need:

  • 1200g of Pork Belly
  • 6 cloves of Garlic
  • 6 cloves of Shallot
  • 75-80 ml Soy Sauce
  • 1 Star Anise
  • 2 Bay Leaves
  • 20g White Peppercorn
  • 20g Sichuan pepper*
  • 30g Fennel Seed
  • 40g Brown Sugar*
  • 400ml Rice Wine or Carbonated Water
  • 200ml Hot water

*can be adjusted to individual liking

Steps

  • Step 1

    Cut the pork belly into 1 cm strips. Make sure to cut it in a way that every strip has 3 layers - skin, fat and meat. Like below.

  • Step 2

    Minced the garlic and shallot like a Ninja! And pack the white peppercorn, fennel seeds and Sichuan pepper into an empty tea bag (optional step, but will make it easier to take out the herbs later).

  • Step 3

    Sauté the pork belly in a sauce pan until it’s no longer pink. Add in the garlic, shallot and star anise and sauté until fragrant. Clear a little space in the same pan to melt the brown sugar until big bubbles are formed. Mix the melted brown sugar with the pork belly.

  • Step 4

    Add soy sauce and sauté the mixture until the soy sauce are absorbed by the pork belly. Move the mixture into a deeper pot to marinate.

  • Step 5

    Using the sauce pan from previous steps, add in the rice wine or the carbonated water and bring it boil. Use spatula to scrape any left over bits from the previous steps.

  • Step 6

    Add step 5 into the same pot as the pork belly mixture. Add bay leaves and the tea bag with herbs. Make sure to submerge the tea bag under the pork belly. Add water and bring it to boil over medium heat.

  • Step 7

    Once it boils, reduce to low heat, and cover it with lid. Cook the mixture for approximately 35 - 40 minutes. You can move it to the fridge afterwards, as it will have a richer taste after it’s been chilled for 24 hours. But I usually can’t wait that long and just eat it immediately after.

Common Side Dishes

  • Marinated Egg

    Peel some hard boil eggs and put it in the braised pork mixture on Step 5 and let it simmer with the sauce.

  • Bok Choy

    Boil the bok choy in chicken stock or cook it however you like!

Make sure you cook extra rice to go with this dish as you will definitely want to go back for a second serving. This also taste great over noodles!
